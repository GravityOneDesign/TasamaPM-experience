## 1. Brand Foundation

### Brand idea

TASAMA is positioned as a business partner that supports client growth through scalable, competitive platforms driven by innovation and technology. The visual system should communicate:

- Growth and elevation
- Modernity and operational confidence
- Structured, technology-enabled services
- Saudi relevance with a global business tone

Source: Brand Guidelines, page 3.

### Design principles

| Principle | Meaning | Design behavior |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Elevated | Show upward movement, ambition, and progress. | Use upward-angle imagery, tall structures, vertical composition, large confident headings. |
| Structured | The brand is precise, balanced, and service-oriented. | Use the 10-divider grid, clean alignment, restrained type hierarchy, and modular layouts. |
| Innovative | TASAMA is technology-led and future-facing. | Use modern devices, network references, wireframe patterning, and clean digital UI cues. |
| Premium but accessible | The brand should feel official and trustworthy without becoming cold. | Pair deep blue with white space, gold accents, soft green, and simple human-readable layouts. |

## 2. Logo System

Source: Brand Guidelines, pages 5-13.

### Logo versions

| Version | Usage |
| --- | --- |
| Primary logo | Default and most recognizable logo. Use across most platforms, presentations, signage, collateral, and digital applications. |
| Full logo | Formal applications where complete brand identification is required, such as official documents and legal or corporate materials. |
| Black logo | Use when color reproduction is limited or when the logo needs to sit on a light monochrome layout. |
| White logo | Use on TASAMA Blue, black, or dark photographic backgrounds. |
| Logo mark | Use in small applications where the full wordmark is not practical, including favicons, social profile images, app icons, and very small printed placements. |

### Clear space

Use the triangle inside the letter "A" as the clear-space unit.

- Keep at least one triangle-unit of clear space around the logo.
- No page margins, text, objects, images, or graphic elements should enter this area.
- Prefer more than the minimum clear space on premium or executive applications.

Source: Brand Guidelines, page 9.

### Partner and endorsement lockups

- Partner logos sit to the right of the TASAMA logo.
- Use a small vertical divider between TASAMA and partner logos.
- Divider height equals four triangle-units from the clear-space system.
- Maintain minimum clear space between both marks and around the full lockup.

Source: Brand Guidelines, page 10.

### Brand architecture

Subsidiary brands may be placed:

- To the right of the TASAMA logo in horizontal layouts.
- Below the TASAMA logo in vertical or narrow layouts.

Both layouts must keep TASAMA visually primary and the subsidiary label clearly subordinate.

Source: Brand Guidelines, page 12.

### Logo misuse

Do not:

- Change the logo color outside approved versions.
- Tilt the logo.
- Distort, stretch, or compress it.
- Modify the size relationship between logo elements.
- Add drop shadows, glows, outlines, or visual effects.
- Recreate the logo with live type.

Source: Brand Guidelines, page 11.

- Use Sky Blue for light information and chart variety.
- Use Energy Orange only for deliberate emphasis.
- Use Charcoal instead of pure black for large dark UI or presentation panels when a softer executive tone is needed.
- Avoid harsh saturated colors outside the approved palette.

| Social post | Blue or Green field with White text, pattern crop, and one strong content focal point. |
| Signage | High contrast: Blue/White, Blue/Green, or White/Charcoal with a single accent. |

### Typography rules

- Use clear hierarchy with only two or three type sizes per layout.
- Prefer sentence case for editorial clarity; use uppercase for compact labels and navigation only.
- Keep body copy concise in presentations. Use short lines and generous white space.
- For bilingual layouts, align the Arabic and English reading directions correctly and keep weights visually balanced.
- Do not stretch, outline, shadow, or decoratively distort type.

## 5. Layout System

Source: Brand Guidelines, pages 15-17.

### Grid

The brand layout grid consists of ten dividers. Use it as the base for:

- Placing logo and footer elements.
- Splitting text and image zones.
- Creating vertical poster and social layouts.
- Cropping the pattern and imagery.
- Balancing white space against blue brand fields.

### Layout behavior

- Use strong alignment and wide white space.
- Pair large brand fields with smaller structured text blocks.
- Use imagery strategically to create contrast, not decoration.
- Keep layouts clean enough that the logo and headline remain readable at a glance.
- Use the pattern as a background structure, not as the primary message.

### Presentation canvas

| Property | Value |
| --- | --- |
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 |
| Source template size | 960 x 540 pt |
| Common outer margin | 48-50 pt |
| Common content start | 88 pt from left |
| Header logo position | Top-left |
| Footer title | Bottom-left |
| Page number | Bottom-right |

The presentation template uses a consistent top-left TASAMA logo, bottom-left presentation title, and bottom-right page number on most standard slides.

## 6. Imagery System

Source: Brand Guidelines, pages 26-27.

### Image style

The imagery style is based on upward perspective. Most visuals should use upward angles to symbolize:

- Growth
- Ambition
- Progress
- Forward movement
- Future-focused energy

### Imagery do list

Use images that:

- Feel clean, modern, and professional.
- Show innovation, progress, and business transformation.
- Highlight upward movement through tall buildings, mountains, trees, or elevated angles.
- Include modern technology, devices, networks, or digital infrastructure.
- Balance nature and urban environments.
- Harmonize with dark blue, green, and light blue.
- Include subtle Saudi context through landscapes, architecture, or business environments.

### Imagery avoid list

Avoid images that:

- Are overly complex, cluttered, or visually busy.
- Feel dark, heavy, or pessimistic.
- Show outdated technology or dated business practices.
- Use generic handshake, boardroom, or group-meeting stock imagery.
- Include bright, harsh, or oversaturated colors.
- Rely on generic plant icons, arrows, or growth cliches without context.

## 7. Pattern System

Source: Brand Guidelines, page 30.

### Pattern concept

The TASAMA pattern is inspired by a wireframe 3D "T" composed from squares and circles. It represents the brand's interconnected services and structured approach.

### Pattern usage

- Crop the pattern freely within layouts.
- Use it as a background or structural layer.
- Keep the line work thin and low-contrast when behind text.
- Use it in Blue, Green, Black, or very light neutral depending on context.
- Combine it with large blue fields or white editorial space.

### Pattern restrictions

- Do not let the pattern compete with the logo.
- Do not place dense pattern details behind small body text.
- Do not recolor it with colors outside the brand palette.
- Do not distort the geometry.

## 8. Icon System

Source: Brand Guidelines, page 29 and Presentation Template, page 44.

### Icon style

The icon system is inspired by the TASAMA logo. Icons use:

- Stroke-line construction.
- Smooth outer curves.
- Sharper inner corners.
- Simple geometric structure.
- Minimal detail.

### Icon rules

- Use outline icons as the default.
- Keep stroke weight consistent within the same layout.
- Use Black, Charcoal, TASAMA Blue, or White depending on the background.
- Use Green, Gold, Sky Blue, or Orange only as secondary accents.
- Avoid filled, illustrative, cartoon-like, or highly detailed icons.
- Pair icons with short labels when meaning is not immediately obvious.

### Recommended icon sizes

| Context | Size |
| --- | --- |
| Small UI icon | 16-20 px |
| Standard UI or slide icon | 24-32 px |
| Feature icon | 40-56 px |
| Hero/icon specimen | 80 px and above |

## 9. Buttons and Navigation

Source: Presentation Template, page 40.

### Button types

| Type | Style | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Main topic button | Filled pill, often Blue/Gold, Gold/Deep Green, or Black/Charcoal. | Primary navigation and main topic selection in decks. |
| Sub-topic button | Thin outline pill using the matching parent color. | Secondary navigation. |
| Back button | Compact filled rectangle in TASAMA Blue. | Return or previous-state control. |
| Section tab | Horizontal tabs with active Blue and muted inactive states. | Deck navigation between sections. |
| Inline link button | Small outline pill. | Secondary calls to action in examples. |

### Button rules

- Keep labels short and uppercase in presentation navigation.
- Use filled buttons for primary commands and outline buttons for secondary commands.
- Use generous horizontal padding.
- Keep button groups aligned on the grid.
- Do not mix more than three button color systems on one slide or screen.

## 10. Data Visualization

Source: Presentation Template, pages 35, 41-43.

### Chart palette

Use the palette in this order for charts:

1. TASAMA Blue `#10069F`
2. Growth Green `#ADDC91`
3. Antique Gold `#B59E5F`
4. Sky Blue `#8DC8E8`
5. Energy Orange `#E87722`
6. Deep Green `#173F35`
7. Charcoal `#1D252D`

### Chart rules

- Use White or very light neutral backgrounds.
- Keep axes, grid lines, and table dividers subtle.
- Label values directly when possible.
- Use donut and radial charts for high-level percentage stories.
- Use bar and stacked bar charts for comparison.
- Use line charts for time-series movement.
- Use color consistently across a presentation.
- Avoid unnecessary chart decoration.

## 13. Accessibility and Production Notes

### Digital accessibility

- Use White text on TASAMA Blue for primary brand areas.
- Use TASAMA Blue or Black text on White for high readability.
- Avoid Antique Gold as small body text on White; use it for accents, dividers, or large type.
- Avoid low-contrast Green text on White for small sizes.
- Check all chart labels for contrast, especially Sky Blue and Gold.

### Print and production

- Use Pantone values where spot color is required.
- Use CMYK values from the brand guideline for print.
- Use HEX/RGB values for digital products and presentations.
- Preserve logo vector artwork from the approved source files.
- Do not manually trace or rebuild the logo.
